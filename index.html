Projects

Smart Shower Meter

To fulfill the requirements of ELEC3117 (Electrical Engineering Design at UNSW), my partner and I set out to build a device that would enable users to monitor and automatically manage their household water consumption in the shower. By the end of the 10 week term we were allocated to build the SSM, it featured the ability to:

  • Measure and display water usage, cost (estimated), temperature, and flowrate in realtime.
  • Display historical metrics graphically on any smartphone or computer via a websocket server.
  • Automatically cut-off water flow when a preset timing or volume limit is reached
    • An audio-visual alert system was implemented to notify shower users when limits were being approached.
  • Calculate battery charge levels and notify users when batteries are in need of replacement.

An ESP32 was used as the microcontroller and I designed and assembled a 4-layer surface-mount PCB for the project using EasyEDA.

Application Specific Processor

As part of COMP3211 (Computer Architecture at UNSW) I worked with a small team to build an Application Specific Processor (ASP) in Verilog using Vivado. The purpose of the ASP was to provide double-layer protection between a processor system and a network to ensure that data received from a network has not been interfered with.

We successfully built a 4-stage pipelined processor with a 16-bit instruction set architecture and verified its functionality through simulation in Vivado.

QuantSoc Website

Since December 2023 I have been involved in UNSW QuantSoc (Quantitiative Finance and Trading Society) as a technical director. In this role I have had the responsibility to co-manage a small team of technical subcommittee members whose purpose is to maintain and update the society website and mock trading engine (amongst other side projects).

To improve the usability of the website by events directors and society executives, in March of 2024 I implemented an admin dashboard, enabling approved users to add events and job listings to the website. I utilised Firebase for Google account authentication and a Firestore (NoSQL) database.
